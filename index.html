About Me

Hi! I’m currently an M.S. student at Cornell University advised by Prof. Kirstin H. Petersen focusing on mechanical engineering and robotics.

I enjoy early-stage, problem-driven work, especially projects involving mechanical design, robotics development, prototyping, and testing. I like thinking at the system level, where mechanics, controls, and real-world constraints come together. I’m drawn to projects that are hard and open-ended, where progress comes from brainstorming, iterating, debugging, and making thoughtful tradeoffs.

Before Cornell, I earned my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Michigan State University. I’m currently seeking internship and early-career opportunities where I can contribute to building and developing real mechanical or robotic systems, from concept through implementation.
